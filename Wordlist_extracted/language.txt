n	(countable) A body of words, and set of methods of combining them (called a grammar), understood by a community and used as a form of communication. 
n	(uncountable) The ability to communicate using words. 
n	(uncountable) A sublanguage: the slang of a particular community or jargon of a particular specialist field. 
n	(countable, uncountable, figuratively) The expression of thought (the communication of meaning) in a specified way; that which communicates something, as language does. 
n	(countable, uncountable) A body of sounds, signs and/or signals by which animals communicate, and by which plants are sometimes also thought to communicate. 
n	(computing, countable) A computer language; a machine language. 
n	(uncountable) Manner of expression. 
n	(uncountable) The particular words used in a speech or a passage of text. 
n	(uncountable) Profanity. 
v	(rare, now nonstandard or technical) To communicate by language; to express in language. 
n	A languet, a flat plate in or below the flue pipe of an organ.
