n	(Canada, US and historical) A department of local (usually municipal) government responsible for general law enforcement. 
n	(UK) A branch of the Home Office responsible for general law enforcement within a specific territory. 
n	(Australia, New Zealand) Any of the formally enacted law enforcement agencies at various levels of government. 
n	(usually plural only) The staff of such a department or agency, particularly its officers; (regional, chiefly US, Caribbean, Jamaica, Scotland, countable) an individual police officer. 
n	(figuratively, usually ironic and mildly derogatory) People who try to enforce norms or standards as if granted authority similar to the police. 
n	(military, slang) Cleanup of a military facility, as a formal duty. 
n	(archaic, now rare) Synonym of administration, the regulation of a community or society. 
v	(transitive) To enforce the law and keep order among (a group). 
v	(transitive, intransitive, military, slang) To clean up an area. 
v	(transitive, figuratively) To enforce norms or standards upon. 
n	(obsolete) Alternative form of policy. [A principle of behaviour, conduct etc. thought to be desirable or necessary, especially as formally expressed by a government or other authoritative body.] 
n	(obsolete) Alternative form of polity, civilization, a regulated community. [(politics) An organizational structure of the government of a state, church, etc.]
