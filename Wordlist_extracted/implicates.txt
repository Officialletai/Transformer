v	(transitive, with “in”) To show to be connected or involved in an unfavorable or criminal way. 
v	(transitive, nonstandard) To imply, to have as a necessary consequence or accompaniment. 
v	(pragmatics) To imply without entailing; to have as an implicature. 
v	(archaic) To fold or twist together, intertwine, interlace, entangle, entwine. 
n	(philosophy) The thing implied.
