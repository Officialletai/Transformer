n	(law, historical) A right to the use of a superior's land as a stipend for certain services to be performed, typically military service. 
n	(law, historical) Synonym of fief: the land so held. 
n	(law, historical) An inheritable estate in land held of a feudal lord on condition of performance of certain services, typically military service. 
n	(law) An inheritable estate in land, whether absolute and without limitation to potential heirs (fee simple) or with limitations to particular kinds of heirs (fee tail). 
n	(figuratively, obsolete) Synonym of possession. 
n	(obsolete) Money paid or bestowed; payment; emolument. 
n	(obsolete) A prize or reward. Only used in the set phrase "A finder's fee" in Modern English. 
n	An additional monetary payment charged for a service or good that is minor compared to the underlying cost. 
v	To reward for services performed, or to be performed; to recompense; to hire or keep in hire; hence, to bribe.
