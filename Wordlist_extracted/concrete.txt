adj	Real, actual, tangible. 
adj	Being or applying to actual things, not abstract qualities or categories. 
adj	Particular, specific, rather than general. 
adj	United by coalescence of separate particles, or liquid, into one mass or solid. 
adj	(modifying a noun, not comparable) Made of concrete, a building material. 
n	(obsolete) A solid mass formed by the coalescence of separate particles; a compound substance, a concretion. 
n	Specifically, a building material created by mixing cement, water, and aggregate such as gravel and sand. 
n	(logic) A term designating both a quality and the subject in which it exists; a concrete term. 
n	Sugar boiled down from cane juice to a solid mass. 
n	(US) A dessert of frozen custard with various toppings. 
n	(chemistry) An extract of herbal materials that has a semi-solid consistency, especially when such materials are partly aromatic. 
v	(usually transitive) To cover with or encase in concrete (building material). 
v	(usually transitive) To solidify: to change from being abstract to being concrete (actual, real). 
v	(intransitive, obsolete) To unite or coalesce into a mass or a solid body.
