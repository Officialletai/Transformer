n	A portal of entry into a building, room, or vehicle, consisting of a rigid plane movable on a hinge. Doors are frequently made of wood or metal. May have a handle to help open and close, a latch to hold the door closed, and a lock that ensures the door cannot be opened without the key. 
n	Any flap, etc. that opens like a door. 
n	(immigration) An entry point. 
n	(figuratively) A means of approach or access. 
n	(figuratively) A possibility. 
n	(figuratively) A barrier. 
n	(computing, dated) A software mechanism by which a user can interact with a program running remotely on a bulletin board system. See BBS door. 
n	The proceeds from entrance fees and/or ticket sales at a venue such as a bar or nightclub, especially in relation to portion paid to the entertainers. "The bar owner gives each band a percentage of the door and charges customers more to get in" 
v	(transitive, cycling) To cause a collision by opening the door of a vehicle in front of an oncoming cyclist or pedestrian.
