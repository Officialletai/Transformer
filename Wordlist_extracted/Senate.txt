n	In some bicameral legislative systems, the upper house or chamber. 
n	A group of experienced, respected, wise individuals serving as decision makers or advisors in a political system or in institutional governance, as in a university, and traditionally of advanced age and male. 
n	Any of several legislative bodies. (In countries which have multiple legislative bodies, the Senate is often the more senior.) 
n	(historical) The council that governed the ancient Roman Republic. 
n	(Canada) The Senate of Canada; the upper house of the Canadian parliament; "the Senate". 
n	(US) The United States Senate. 
n	(US) The upper legislative body of many of the states of the United States.
