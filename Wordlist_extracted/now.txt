adj	Present; current. 
adj	(informal) Fashionable; popular; up to date; current. 
adj	(archaic, law) At the time the will is written. Used in order to prevent any inheritance from being transferred to a person of a future marriage. Does not indicate the existence of a previous marriage. 
adv	At the present time. 
adv	(sentential) Used to introduce a point, a qualification of what has previously been said, a remonstration or a rebuke. 
adv	Differently from the immediate past; differently from a more remote past or a possible future; differently from all other times. 
adv	At the time reached within a narration. 
adv	Used to indicate a context of urgency. 
adv	(obsolete) As 'but now': Very recently; not long ago; up to the present. 
adv	Used to address a switching side, or sharp change in attitude from before. (In this usage, now is usually emphasized). 
adv	Sometimes; occasionally. 
n	(uncountable) The present time. 
n	(often with "the") The state of not paying attention to the future or the past. 
n	(countable, chiefly in phenomenology) A particular instant in time, as perceived at that instant. 
v	Misspelling of know. [(transitive) To perceive the truth or factuality of; to be certain of or that.] 
n	(US) Acronym of National Organization for Women.
