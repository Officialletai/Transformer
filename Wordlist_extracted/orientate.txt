v	(UK, New Zealand, Australia, intransitive) To face a given direction. 
v	(UK, New Zealand, Australia, reflexive) To determine one's position relative to the surroundings; to orient (oneself). 
v	To arrange in order; to dispose or place (a body) so as to show its relation to other bodies, or the relation of its parts among themselves. 
v	(UK, New Zealand, Australia, transitive) To position (something), to align relative to a given position. 
v	(archaic) To move or turn toward the east; to veer from the north or south toward the east.
