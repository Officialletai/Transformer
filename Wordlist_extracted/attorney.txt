n	(US) A lawyer; one who advises or represents others in legal matters as a profession. 
n	(UK, dated, 19th century and earlier) One such who practised in the courts of the common law. 
n	(UK, 20th century and later, rare, usually derogatory) A solicitor. 
n	(obsolete outside set phrases) An agent or representative authorized to act on someone else's behalf. 
n	(Philippines, sometimes US) An honorific given to lawyers and notaries public, or those holders by profession who also do other jobs. Usually capitalized or abbreviated as Atty. 
v	(intransitive, rare) To work as a legal attorney. 
v	(transitive, rare) To provide with a legal attorney.
