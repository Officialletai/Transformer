n	A loud burst of voice or voices; a violent and sudden outcry, especially that of a multitude expressing joy, triumph, exultation, anger, or great effort. 
n	(UK, Australia, New Zealand, slang) A round of drinks in a pub; the turn to pay the shot or scot; an act of paying for a round of drinks. 
n	(UK, Australia, slang) A call-out for an emergency services team. 
n	(informal) A greeting, name-check or other mention, for example on a radio or TV programme. 
n	(informal) A suggestion; an idea. 
v	(intransitive) To utter a sudden and loud cry, as in joy, triumph, exultation or anger, or to attract attention, to animate others, etc. 
v	(transitive) To utter with a shout; to cry; to shout out 
v	(Australia, New Zealand, slang) To pay for food, drink or entertainment for others. 
v	(Internet) To post a text message (for example, email) in upper case, regarded as the electronic messaging equivalent of oral shouting. 
v	(transitive, obsolete) To treat with shouts or clamor. 
n	(UK, dialect) A light flat-bottomed boat used in duck-shooting.
