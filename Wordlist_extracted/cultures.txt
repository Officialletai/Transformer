n	The arts, customs, lifestyles, background, and habits that characterize humankind, or a particular society or nation. 
n	The beliefs, values, behaviour and material objects that constitute a people's way of life. 
n	The conventional conducts and ideologies of a community; the system comprising the accepted norms and values of a society. 
n	(anthropology) Any knowledge passed from one generation to the next, not necessarily with respect to human beings. 
n	(botany) Cultivation. 
n	(microbiology) The process of growing a bacterial or other biological entity in an artificial medium. 
n	The growth thus produced. 
n	A group of bacteria. 
n	(cartography) The details on a map that do not represent natural features of the area delineated, such as names and the symbols for towns, roads, meridians, and parallels. 
n	(archaeology) A recurring assemblage of artifacts from a specific time and place that may constitute the material culture remains of a particular past human society. 
n	(euphemistic) Ethnicity, race (and its associated arts, customs, etc.) 
v	(transitive) to maintain in an environment suitable for growth (especially of bacteria) (compare cultivate) 
v	(transitive) to increase the artistic or scientific interest (in something) (compare cultivate)
