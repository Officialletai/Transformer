v	(intransitive) To enter into the conjugal or connubial state; to take a husband or a wife. 
v	(intransitive) To enter into marriage with one another. 
v	(transitive) To take as husband or wife. 
v	(transitive) To arrange for the marriage of; to give away as wife or husband. 
v	(transitive) To unite in wedlock or matrimony; to perform the ceremony of joining spouses; to bring about a marital union according to the laws or customs of a place. 
v	(intransitive, figuratively, of inanimate or abstract things) To join or connect. See also marry up. 
v	(transitive, figuratively) To unite; to join together into a close union. 
v	(nautical) To place (two ropes) alongside each other so that they may be grasped and hauled on at the same time. 
v	(nautical) To join (two ropes) end to end so that both will pass through a block.
