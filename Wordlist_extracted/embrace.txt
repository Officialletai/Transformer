v	(transitive) To clasp (someone or each other) in the arms with affection; to take in the arms; to hug. 
v	(transitive, figuratively) To seize (something) eagerly or with alacrity; to accept or take up with cordiality; to welcome. 
v	(transitive, figuratively) To submit to; to undergo. 
v	(transitive, also figuratively) To encircle; to enclose, to encompass. 
v	(transitive, figuratively) To enfold, to include (ideas, principles, etc.); to encompass. 
v	(transitive, obsolete, rare) To fasten on, as armour. 
v	(transitive, figuratively, obsolete) To accept (someone) as a friend; to accept (someone's) help gladly. 
v	(transitive, law, figuratively, obsolete) To attempt to influence (a court, jury, etc.) corruptly; to practise embracery. 
n	An act of putting arms around someone and bringing the person close to the chest; a hug. 
n	(figuratively) An enclosure partially or fully surrounding someone or something. 
n	(figuratively) Full acceptance (of something). 
n	(figuratively) An act of enfolding or including.
