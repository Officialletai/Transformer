v	(transitive) To wish for or desire (something); to feel a need or desire for; to crave or demand. 
v	(by extension) To make it easy or tempting to do something undesirable, or to make it hard or challenging to refrain from doing it. 
v	(transitive, in particular) To wish, desire, or demand to see, have the presence of or do business with. 
v	(intransitive) To desire (to experience desire); to wish. 
v	(colloquial, usually second person, often future tense) To be advised to do something (compare should, ought). 
v	(transitive, now colloquial) To lack and be in need of or require (something, such as a noun or verbal noun). 
v	(transitive, now rare) To have occasion for (something requisite or useful); to require or need. 
v	(intransitive, dated) To be lacking or deficient or absent. 
v	(intransitive, dated) To be in a state of destitution; to be needy; to lack. 
v	(transitive, archaic) To lack and be without, to not have (something). 
v	(transitive, obsolete, by extension) To lack and (be able to) do without. 
n	(countable) A desire, wish, longing. 
n	(countable, often followed by of) Lack, absence, deficiency. 
n	(uncountable) Poverty. 
n	Something needed or desired; a thing of which the loss is felt. 
n	(UK, mining) A depression in coal strata, hollowed out before the subsequent deposition took place. 
n	(dialectal) A mole (Talpa europea). 
n	A personification of want.
