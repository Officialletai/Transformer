v	simple past tense of underlie 
v	(transitive) To lay (something) underneath something else; to put under. 
v	(transitive) To provide a support for something; to raise or support by something laid under. 
v	(transitive) To put a tap on (a shoe). 
v	(mining, transitive, intransitive, of a vein, fault, or lode) To incline from the vertical. 
n	A layer (of earth, etc.) that lies under another; substratum. 
n	A soft floor covering that lies under a carpet. 
n	Anything that is underlaid. 
n	(printing, historical) A piece of paper pasted under woodcuts, stereotype plates, etc. in a form, to bring them up to the necessary level for printing. 
n	(music) Lyrics; or more specifically, the way in which lyrics are assigned to musical notes.
