n	(uncountable) A black or brownish black rock formed from prehistoric plant remains, composed largely of carbon and burned as a fuel. 
n	(countable) A type of coal, such as bituminous, anthracite, or lignite, and grades and varieties thereof, as a fuel commodity ready to buy and burn. 
n	(countable) A piece of coal used for burning (this use is less common in American English) 
n	(countable) A glowing or charred piece of coal, wood, or other solid fuel. 
n	Charcoal. 
v	(intransitive) To take on a supply of coal (usually of steam ships). 
v	(transitive) To supply with coal. 
v	(intransitive) To be converted to charcoal. 
v	(transitive) To burn to charcoal; to char. 
v	(transitive) To mark or delineate with charcoal.
