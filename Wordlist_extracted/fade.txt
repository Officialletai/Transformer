adj	(archaic) Weak; insipid; tasteless. 
n	(golf) A golf shot that curves intentionally to the player's right (if they are right-handed) or to the left (if left-handed). 
n	A haircut where the hair is short or shaved on the sides of the head and longer on top. See also high-top fade and low fade. 
n	(slang) A fight. 
n	(music, cinematography) A gradual decrease in the brightness of a shot or the volume of sound or music (as a means of cutting to a new scene or starting a new song). 
n	(slang) The act of disappearing from a place so as not to be found; covert departure. 
v	(transitive, golf) To hit the ball with the shot called a fade. 
v	(intransitive) To grow weak; to lose strength; to decay; to perish gradually; to wither, as a plant. 
v	(intransitive) To lose freshness, color, or brightness; to become faint in hue or tint; hence, to be wanting in color. 
v	(intransitive) To sink away; to disappear gradually; to grow dim; to vanish. 
v	(transitive) To cause to fade. 
v	(transitive, gambling) To bet against. 
adj	(archaic) Strong; bold; doughty.
