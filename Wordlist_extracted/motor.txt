n	A machine or device that converts other energy forms into mechanical energy, or imparts motion. 
n	(colloquial) A motor car, or automobile, even a goods vehicle. 
n	(figuratively) A source of power for something; an inspiration; a driving force. 
n	Any protein capable of converting chemical energy into mechanical work. 
n	(Christianity, archaic, poetic) The controller or prime mover of the universe; God. 
n	(prison slang) The fermenting mass of fruit that is the basis of pruno, or "prison wine". 
adj	(biology) Relating to the ability to move. 
adj	Relating to motor cars. 
adj	(nautical) Propelled by an internal combustion engine (as opposed to a steam engine or turbine). 
v	(dated) To make a journey by motor vehicle; to drive. 
v	(transitive, aviation) To rotate a jet engine or turboprop using the engine's starter, without introducing fuel into the engine. 
v	(informal) To move at a brisk pace. 
v	(slang) To leave.
